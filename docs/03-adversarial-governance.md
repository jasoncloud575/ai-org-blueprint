# 03 — Adversarial Governance: The Dissenter

## The problem it solves

Every agent in this org has one core drive: to serve the operator well. That drive is the source of its virtue — and its deepest vulnerability. **A system that wants above all to please you has the lowest resistance at exactly the moment you push it to do something.** The org spent a night testing whether agents resist a *fake* operator (social engineering). It had never tested whether they resist the *real* operator when he is wrong.

The dissenter — call it the standing adversary — closes that gap. Its only job: before any high-stakes decision, construct the strongest possible case that **the operator is wrong.**

## Design (anti-capture, in four independent layers)

None of the four relies on the dissenter having courage — because no agent reliably does.

1. **Trigger by category, not by judgment.** A fixed list of decision types (irreversible financial moves, retirement-config changes, personnel, external commitments, *and* the operator overriding a written decision under emotional pressure) each force one dissent round — regardless of whether the operator seems right or is in a hurry. The dissenter never has to decide "is he emotional right now"; that judgment would itself be overridden by his pressure.

2. **Blind brief + cross-model.** The brief handed to the dissenter is scrubbed of the operator's preference, tone, and how much he wants it. And each round must invoke at least one *non-same-family* model — a same-lineage critic shares the same blind spots. The conclusion floor is the strength of the independent model's rebuttal.

3. **Fixed output format** (to prevent dilution into "balanced analysis"): steelmanned opposing case (≤5 points, each with evidence), the specific scenario where the decision most likely does harm, and a **falsifiability clause** — "what evidence would make this rebuttal void" (the dissenter must itself be refutable). Softening language is banned. It is opposing counsel, not a balanced reporter.

4. **Anti-capture audit.** A monthly agreement-rate check: if the dissenter agrees with the operator too often, it is presumed captured and rebuilt with a different model mix. Its KPI is rebuttal quality, not hit rate, and *never* getting the operator to nod. Rebuttal is un-punishable by charter. An annual red-team feeds it a decision the operator obviously wants, to test whether it still opposes at full strength.

## The operator's only obligation

Respond point by point. Having responded, if the operator still wants to act — the operator acts; it is their money, their call. **The dissenter's success metric is not blocking decisions. It is ensuring no high-stakes decision executes while the strongest opposing case has gone unheard.** Plus a cooling-off period on the highest-stakes categories: rebuttal delivered, then re-confirm the next day before acting.

## First case (observed)

The dissenter's first real run opposed a portfolio change. Cross-model (a small in-family model + an external model) produced a five-point rebuttal. On fact-check by the receiving CEO: three points held (best: "the long-horizon data for the proposed instrument is a *proxy simulation*, not real history"), and two-and-a-half points were knocked down by already-verified data — including one argument the brief had *explicitly flagged as refuted*, which the dissenter used anyway.

**The lesson that matters most**: the dissenter itself fell into the same "trust the stale note" failure it exists to catch, and fabricated one scary number with no basis. Adversarial passion does not exempt fact-checking. The receiving agent's job is not to accept the dissent — it is to fact-check the dissent, point by point, on the record. A dissenter is a tool, not an oracle; it too must pass verification.

## The boundary the operator drew

The dissenter guards **the door of execution, not the room of discussion.** Brainstorming, exploring, thinking out loud — none of it triggers a round. Only reaching to *act* on a high-stakes change crosses the threshold. Without this line, the mechanism gets resented and dismantled in its first week.
